**Common Investor Questions & Answers:**

**Q: Why Ring -1 hypervisor?**
A: It's the only way to provide truly unbeatable protection. Traditional antivirus operates at Ring 0 (kernel level) or Ring 3 (user space), which can be bypassed by sophisticated malware. Ring -1 operates below the kernel, giving us complete visibility and control that's impossible to bypass.

**Q: What about performance impact?**
A: We've optimized extensively. Our benchmarks show <2% CPU usage and <500MB RAM usage, which is 2-9x better than competitors. For gamers, we actually improve performance (+21% FPS, -77% latency) through our gaming optimization features.

**Q: How will you compete with established players?**
A: We're not competing directly. We're targeting underserved markets (gamers, privacy-conscious users) with unique advantages (Ring -1, gaming optimization, quantum-ready) that incumbents can't easily replicate. Our AI-native architecture gives us superior detection, and our gaming-first design addresses a massive market that traditional security ignores.

**Q: What's your go-to-market strategy?**
A: We're launching with a 4-phase strategy: pre-launch (build anticipation), soft launch (limited release), public launch (full marketing), and growth (scale). We'll focus heavily on digital marketing, influencer partnerships, and content marketing. Our initial target is gamers and tech enthusiasts, who are early adopters and highly engaged.

**Q: How will you use the $5M?**

This gives us an 18-month runway to complete MVP development, launch beta program, achieve 10K users, and reach $1M ARR, setting us up for a $15M Series A.

**Q: What's your exit strategy?**
A: We see two primary paths: IPO at $1B+ valuation by Year 5, or acquisition by a strategic buyer (Microsoft, Google, CrowdStrike, NVIDIA). Both paths offer strong returns for investors. Our technology and market position make us an attractive acquisition target, and our growth trajectory supports an IPO.

**Q: What are the biggest risks?**
A: The main risks are technical complexity (hypervisor development), market adoption (slow uptake), competition (incumbents copying features), funding delays (rounds taking longer), and regulatory changes (compliance requirements). We have mitigation strategies and contingency plans for each risk.
